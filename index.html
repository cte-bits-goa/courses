About US

  • Courses and Workshops

    CTE hosts courses and workshops every semester which are taught by the members of our campus These courses are held to enhance and develop the technical skills of the students of our campus and to help build a rich tech culture.

  • AAP

    Academic Assistance Program or AAP is undertaken by CTE to help juniors with their academics. Mentors are assigned a batch of students to help and guide. AAP is in no form meant to replace classes taken by Professors. 

  • TIP

    TIP is a formal platform for students to discover projects that they can work on and collaborators with others. Following a peer to peer mentorship model, it allows students to explore their interests under the guidance of experienced mentors.

  • Tech Weekend

    Tech Weekend is an event organized by CTE every semester in which Students participate in various Tech related competitions held by different Tech Clubs to try and win a handsome cash prize.

  • Project Funding

    CTE provides financial funding to the Technical Clubs and Projects of the campus to support their activities.

Acapella Music Production

Synchronoise

Handout

A cappella is basically singing without musical instrument accompaniment, but the word itself came from the Italian language, and it means "In the style of the chapel." This is a 4-week learning experience designed to teach you everything we know about acapella. The course is structured to give you an in-depth understanding of acapella arrangements. You will get to explore and appreciate the entire creative process of acapella production from start to finish. You'll learn vocal techniques, crafting harmonies, structuring arrangements, blending voice parts, beatboxing and vocal effects, ear candy, performance tips, recording vocals, and a lot more. You will also be arranging your own acapella song along with us by the end of this course.
Course Outcomes:
● Understand and appreciate Acapella music and its arrangement
● Create your first acapella cover song

Introduction and Applications of Machine Learning

SAiDL

Handout

The course intends to help students with no experience in machine learning take their
first steps in the field. We will start off with the basic Machine learning theory and look
into essential tools needed for ML; libraries like numpy and pandas. Then we will
proceed to teach the students about various machine learning algorithms, the maths
behind it. With the help of scikit learn, the students will learn how to implement various
models as well.

DoPY

Handout

Autonomous Maze solving in ROS

ERC

Handout

Autonomous robots, just like humans, also have the ability to make their own
decisions and then perform an action accordingly. A truly autonomous robot is one
that can perceive its environment, make decisions based on what it perceives and/or
has been programmed to recognize conditions, and then actuate a movement or
manipulation within that environment. In this workshop, we will go over some
important concepts like planning, controls, and computer vision required to create a
fully autonomous stack for robot navigation. We will cover some important skills like
ROS and OpenCV. At the end of the course, students will create a simulation of a fully
autonomous robot navigating through a maze.

Theory of Relativity

Handout

Theory of Relativity is undoubtedly one of the most radical theories that was offered
to physics, filled with common misconceptions, in this workshop we try to understand what the theory is really
all about, what it has to say and how are the most common paradoxes resolved. This workshop mainly
attempts to give an introductory understanding to The Special Theory of Relativity, its postulates and its
extraordinary results and outcomes.
